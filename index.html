Our Commitment

Built with
select grade hardwoods.

We build our cabinets with a variety of select grade hardwoods. Our expert craftsmen carefully select and grain match our doors, drawer fronts, and face frames producing an unparalleled beauty that is unmistakably Mingo Mountain.

We use domestic maple plywood for our cabinet boxes and provide furniture quality, solid maple dovetailed drawers with high end runners. We choose the highest quality hinges, drawer slides, and other components to ensure a lifetime of trouble-free use.

We finish our cabinets with conversion varnish in an unlimited variety of stain tints, solid colors, glazes, and distresses. Our unique finishes require painstaking and patient handwork — the only way to create the beautiful finishes for which we are known. Mingo Mountain will do an expert installation, and leave you with a completely finished job.

How We Work

Our process,
your vision.

We believe the best work comes from close collaboration. Here is how we turn your vision into a finished piece you will have for life.

01
Consultation

We begin with a conversation — learning about your space, your lifestyle, your tastes, and your goals. No pressure, no sales pitch. Just genuine listening from craftsmen who love what they do.

02
Design

Together, we develop a plan that is both beautiful and functional — selecting wood species, door styles, finishes, hardware, and layout to reflect who you are and how you live.

03
Material Selection

We personally select the hardwood lumber for your project, grain matching doors and drawer fronts for a seamless, flowing aesthetic. The right material makes all the difference.

04
Construction

Every piece is built by hand in our Kettle Falls studio using select hardwoods, maple plywood boxes, dovetailed drawers, and premium hardware. James and Russ personally oversee every stage.

05
Finishing

Our conversion varnish finishing process requires painstaking, patient handwork. Stain tints, glazes, distresses, and solid colors — each piece receives multiple coats and careful sanding between applications.

06
Quality Check

Before anything leaves our shop, every door, drawer, and cabinet is thoroughly inspected. Soft-close hardware is adjusted, finishes are reviewed, and dimensions are verified against your plans.

07
Installation

We handle the complete installation ourselves — so you can be confident the finished result perfectly matches the vision, down to the very last detail. We leave your home clean and completely finished.

08
Aftercare

Our relationship doesn't end at installation. We stand behind our work and are always available for adjustments, questions, or future additions to your home. Our work is built to last a lifetime.

The Craftsmen

A father and son
built on craft.

Mingo Mountain Woodworking is a father and son business. James Fish is the founder and master craftsman, with decades of fine woodworking experience behind every piece he builds. His son Russ now runs the company in close collaboration with his father, carrying the same exacting standards forward.

Russ Fish
Partner & Craftsman

Russ Fish is the son of founder James Fish, and learned the trade working alongside his father in the Mingo Mountain shop. He has developed into a skilled craftsman and now leads the company as a second generation owner.

Russ brings fresh energy and a trained eye to every project, working in close collaboration with James to ensure that each kitchen, vanity, and built-in that leaves the shop meets the exacting standards that Mingo Mountain has always been known for.

James Fish
Founder & Master Craftsman

James Fish founded Mingo Mountain Woodworking after a career working across the full range of the building trades. Timber framing, general contracting, finish carpentry, trim work, and custom cabinetry. That breadth of experience shapes everything he builds.

It is this depth of knowledge that sets Mingo Mountain apart. James approaches every cabinet project the way a builder approaches a structure: with a thorough understanding of how spaces work, how materials behave, and how the finest details come together to make something truly built to last.
